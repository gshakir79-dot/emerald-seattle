EMERALD/Day Trips/Mount Rainier

Beyond the City — 2.5 HR DRIVE · FULL DAY

Mount Rainier National Park

That white apparition floating over the skyline is a 14,411-foot glaciated volcano, and it is entirely reasonable to stand on its flank by 10 AM. Paradise — the aptly named south-side meadows — is the day trip against which all others are measured.

✓ Field-checked July 2026

DRIVE~2.5 hrs to Paradise via Ashford
SEASONMeadows peak late Jul–Aug; road open year-round
COST$30/vehicle + timed entry in summer
ALTITUDEParadise sits at 5,400 ft — pack layers

The Plan

7:00

Leave Seattle early

Beat the gate line. Last real coffee and gas are in Eatonville or Ashford — there's neither inside the park.

9:30

Paradise & the Skyline Trail

The full loop is 5.5 miles and earns every view: wildflower meadows, marmots, and the Nisqually Glacier groaning in the distance. Short on legs? Nisqually Vista is a gentle 1.2-mile version.

14:00

Reflection Lakes & Narada Falls

Two pull-offs on the drive down that deliver absurd returns per minute — Rainier mirrored in still water, and a 168-foot waterfall a two-minute walk from the road.

16:30

Copper Creek Inn

Just outside the gate in Ashford. Blackberry pie is the closing argument of this itinerary. Non-negotiable.

Need to know

  • Summer requires a timed-entry reservation for the Paradise corridor in addition to the park fee — book on recreation.gov before you drive.
  • Snow lingers at Paradise into July; trails can be icy when Seattle is sunny. Check conditions and bring real shoes.
  • Cell service dies at the park boundary. Download offline maps before Ashford.
  • Clear-day rule: if the Mountain is out in Seattle, it's spectacular up close. If it's socked in, consider Snoqualmie Falls instead — Paradise in a cloud is a very cold gray room.
