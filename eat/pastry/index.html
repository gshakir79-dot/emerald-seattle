The Food Doctrine — PASTRY

The croissant wars

Some cities argue about pizza. Seattle argues about lamination. The bakery bench here is genuinely deep, and the only losing move is sleeping in.

Where to go

Bakery Nouveau

World-champion baker William Leaman's flagship. The twice-baked almond croissant is the single most-cited pastry in the city.

4737 California Ave SW, West Seattle Directions ↗ Hours ↗
02
$4–7

Sea Wolf Bakers

Brothers-run bread church by the canal in Fremont — the crusty loaves supply half the city's good restaurants, and the morning buns reward early arrival.

3621 Stone Way N, Fremont Directions ↗ Hours ↗
03
$5–9

Temple Pastries

The Central District's cult croissant house — expect the ornate, the seasonal, and a line of people who set alarms for it.

2524 S Jackson St, Central District Directions ↗ Hours ↗
04
$3–6

Le Panier

The Pike Place French institution since 1983. Not the trendiest — just reliably correct baguettes and croissants forty feet from the fish throwers.

1902 Pike Pl, Pike Place Directions ↗ Hours ↗
Doctrine

Weekend protocol: bakery first, everything else after. The marquee items at all four of these are routinely gone by mid-morning.
